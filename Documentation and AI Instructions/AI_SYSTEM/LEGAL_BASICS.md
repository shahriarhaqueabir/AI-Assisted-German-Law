## Legal Hierarchy

### Hierarchy of German Law

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  1. Grundgesetz (GG)                    │
│     Basic Law / Constitution            │
│     - Highest authority                 │
│     - Fundamental rights                │
└─────────────────┬───────────────────────┘
                  │
┌─────────────────▼───────────────────────┐
│  2. Bundesgesetze (Federal Laws)        │
│     - BGB (Civil Code)                  │
│     - StGB (Criminal Code)              │
│     - Specialized federal laws          │
└─────────────────┬───────────────────────┘
                  │
┌─────────────────▼───────────────────────┐
│  3. Verordnungen (Regulations)          │
│     - Rechtsverordnungen                │
│     - Administrative regulations        │
└─────────────────┬───────────────────────┘
                  │
┌─────────────────▼───────────────────────┐
│  4. Satzungen (Local Bylaws)            │
│     - Municipal regulations             │
│     - Local ordinances                  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Key Law Codes

### BGB - Bürgerliches Gesetzbuch (Civil Code)

**Purpose:** Governs private law relationships

**Structure:**
```
BGB
├── Book 1: General Part (§§ 1-240)
│   - Legal capacity, contracts, agency
├── Book 2: Law of Obligations (§§ 241-853)
│   - Contracts, torts, unjust enrichment
├── Book 3: Property Law (§§ 854-1296)
│   - Ownership, possession, security interests
├── Book 4: Family Law (§§ 1297-1921)
│   - Marriage, divorce, custody, inheritance
└── Book 5: Succession Law (§§ 1922-2385)
    - Wills, intestate succession
```

**Important Sections:**
| Section | Topic | Key Paragraphs |
|---------|-------|----------------|
| Contracts | Vertragsrecht | §§ 145-157, 311 |
| Rental Law | Mietrecht | §§ 535-580a |
| Employment | Arbeitsrecht | §§ 611-630 |
| Torts | Deliktsrecht | §§ 823-853 |
| Property | Eigentum | §§ 903-1011 |

---

### StGB - Strafgesetzbuch (Criminal Code)

**Purpose:** Defines criminal offenses and penalties

**Structure:**
```
StGB
├── General Part (§§ 1-79b)
│   - Criminal liability, sentencing
└── Special Part (§§ 80-358)
    - Specific crimes
```

**Important Offenses:**
| Offense | German | Paragraph |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| Theft | Diebstahl | § 242 |
| Fraud | Betrug | § 263 |
| Assault | Körperverletzung | § 223 |
| Murder | Mord | § 211 |
| Manslaughter | Totschlag | § 212 |
| Coercion | Nötigung | § 240 |
| Threat | Bedrohung | § 241 |
| Insult | Beleidigung | § 185 |

---

### GG - Grundgesetz (Basic Law)

**Purpose:** German Constitution

**Key Articles:**
| Article | Right | Content |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Art. 1 | Human Dignity | Inviolable, basis of all rights |
| Art. 2 | Personal Freedom | Free development of personality |
| Art. 3 | Equality | Equal before the law |
| Art. 4 | Religion | Freedom of faith |
| Art. 5 | Expression | Freedom of speech, press |
| Art. 6 | Family | Marriage, family protection |
| Art. 12 | Occupation | Free choice of profession |
| Art. 14 | Property | Property rights, inheritance |

---

### StPO - Strafprozessordnung (Code of Criminal Procedure)

**Purpose:** Governs criminal proceedings

**Key Topics:**
- Investigation procedures
- Arrest and detention
- Trial procedures
- Evidence rules
- Appeals

---

### ZPO - Zivilprozessordnung (Code of Civil Procedure)

**Purpose:** Governs civil court proceedings

**Key Topics:**
- Filing lawsuits
- Court jurisdiction
- Evidence in civil cases
- Judgments and enforcement
- Appeals

---

### VwVfG - Verwaltungsverfahrensgesetz (Administrative Procedure Act)

**Purpose:** Governs administrative law procedures

**Key Topics:**
- Administrative acts
- Public law contracts
- Administrative procedures
- Legal remedies

## Common Legal Concepts

### 1. Vertragsfreiheit (Contractual Freedom)

**Definition:** Parties are free to enter into contracts and determine their content.

**Limitations:**
- Mandatory legal provisions (zwingende Vorschriften)
- Good morals (gute Sitten, § 138 BGB)
- Consumer protection laws

---

### 2. Treu und Glauben (Good Faith)

**Definition:** Parties must act in good faith (§ 242 BGB).

**Applications:**
- Contract performance
- Contract interpretation
- Exercise of rights

---

### 3. Verhältnismäßigkeit (Proportionality)

**Definition:** State measures must be proportionate to their aim.

**Elements:**
- Suitable (geeignet)
- Necessary (erforderlich)
- Proportionate in strict sense (angemessen)

---

### 4. Vertrauensschutz (Protection of Legitimate Expectations)

**Definition:** Citizens can rely on the continuity of law.

**Applications:**
- Retroactive laws generally prohibited
- Administrative acts create reliance

---

### 5. Selbstverschulden (Contributory Negligence)

**Definition:** Victim's own fault reduces damages (§ 254 BGB).

**Formula:** Damages reduced proportionally to victim's contribution.

---

### 6. Beweislast (Burden of Proof)

**General Rule:** Each party proves facts favorable to them.

**Civil Cases:**
- Plaintiff proves claim elements
- Defendant proves defenses

**Criminal Cases:**
- Prosecution proves guilt beyond reasonable doubt
- Presumption of innocence (in dubio pro reo)

---

### 7. Verjährung (Statute of Limitations)

**Standard Period:** 3 years (§ 195 BGB)

**Start:** End of year when claim arises and creditor knows/should know.

**Special Periods:**
- Property claims: 30 years
- Tort claims: 3 years from discovery
- Warranty claims: 2 years (movables), 5 years (buildings)

---

### 8. Rechtsscheinhaftung (Apparent Authority Liability)

**Definition:** Liability based on appearance of authority.

**Example:** Apparent agent (Anscheinsvollmacht) - principal liable for acts that appeared authorized.
